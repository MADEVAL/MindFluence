# Scenario: Product Launch

## CONTEXT
You are planning a product launch — from pre-launch hype through launch day to post-launch follow-up.

## GOAL
Build anticipation, convert early adopters, and maximize launch-day sales while building a base of evangelists.

---

## PHASE 1: PRE-LAUNCH (7–14 days before)

### Bias Stack
`Fear (problem awareness) → Authority (why we built this) → Social Proof (waitlist) → In-Group (early access)`

### Content Types
1. **Problem Awareness Posts** — Vividly describe the pain. Do NOT mention the product. Make the problem feel urgent and personal.
   - *Primary bias: Availability Heuristic* — tell a specific story of someone struggling with this problem.
   - *Secondary: Fear/Loss Aversion* — "This is costing you $X per [day/week/month]."

2. **Origin Story** — Why you're building this. Personal, vulnerable, mission-driven.
   - *Primary bias: Authority* — establish founder/product credibility.
   - *Secondary: In-Group Favoritism* — "We're building this for people like us who..."

3. **Waitlist / Early Access** — Open a waitlist. Share numbers as they grow.
   - *Primary bias: Social Proof* — "2,147 people already on the list."
   - *Secondary: Scarcity* — "First 500 get lifetime discount."
   - *Tertiary: Endowment Effect* — give waitlist members something now (early content, preview, name in credits).

4. **Behind-the-Scenes** — Show the building process. Humanize the product.
   - *Primary bias: Halo Effect* — the quality and care you put in transfers to perceived product quality.

---

## PHASE 2: LAUNCH DAY

### Bias Stack
`Urgency (scarcity) → Anchoring (pricing) → Social Proof (live purchases) → Risk Reversal (guarantee)`

### Content Types
1. **Launch Announcement** — The big reveal. Frame as the solution to the problem you've been building up.
   - *Primary bias: Framing* — "This isn't [category]. This is [new category you're creating]."
   - *Secondary: Authority* — cite the problem research, your credentials, the build process.

2. **Pricing Reveal** — Use anchoring aggressively.
   - Show "what it should cost" (the anchor) → show the actual price.
   - Limited-time launch discount.
   - Bonus stack: add bonuses with assigned dollar values to inflate perceived value.

3. **Social Proof Wave** — Pre-seed testimonials from beta users. Share purchase numbers live.
   - "Just crossed 500 sales in the first hour."

4. **Urgency Injection** — Countdown, limited bonuses, price increase warning.
   - *Primary bias: Loss Aversion* — "Price goes up in 24 hours. Bonus package disappears."
   - *Secondary: Sunk Cost* for those who've been following the pre-launch.

---

## PHASE 3: POST-LAUNCH (3–7 days after)

### Bias Stack
`Confirmation (you made the right choice) → Social Proof (look who else bought) → In-Group (welcome to the community)`

### Content Types
1. **Purchase Justification** — Content that helps buyers feel smart about their decision.
   - Case studies, "what to expect" guides, setup tutorials.
   - *Primary bias: Cognitive Dissonance resolution* — buyers seek evidence they chose right.

2. **Community Welcoming** — Activate the in-group.
   - "Welcome to the 3,200+ who've joined."
   - Private community access.
   - *Primary bias: In-Group Favoritism + Group Polarization* — shared identity strengthens loyalty.

3. **Last-Chance Scarcity** — Final call before price increase / bonus removal.
   - "Doors closing. Last chance at this price."

4. **User-Generated Content Prompt** — Ask new users to share. Their confirmation bias becomes your marketing.

---

## BIAS CHECKLIST FOR LAUNCH
- [ ] Problem made vivid and personal before product revealed
- [ ] Origin story establishes authority and relatability
- [ ] Pricing anchored against higher reference point
- [ ] Social proof visible at every stage (numbers, faces, testimonials)
- [ ] Urgency mechanisms genuine (not fake scarcity)
- [ ] Risk reversed (guarantee, trial, easy cancellation)
- [ ] Post-purchase content feeds confirmation bias
- [ ] Community element (in-group) activated immediately after purchase
