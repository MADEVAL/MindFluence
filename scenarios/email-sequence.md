# Scenario: Email Sequence

## CONTEXT
Writing an email sequence - welcome series, nurture campaign, re-engagement, or cart abandonment.

## GOAL
Move subscribers through a psychological progression: stranger → trust → desire → action → loyalty - using cognitive biases at each stage.

---

## SEQUENCE TYPES

### 1. WELCOME SEQUENCE (3–5 emails over 7–10 days)

**Psychological arc:** Gratitude + expectation-setting → Value + authority → Social proof → Soft pitch → Hard pitch or community invite.

| Email | Purpose | Dominant Bias | Content |
|-------|---------|---------------|---------|
| #1 (immediate) | Deliver lead magnet + set expectations | Reciprocity + Endowment | "Here's what you asked for. Here's what to expect from me." |
| #2 (Day 2) | Best value - no pitch | Authority + Availability | Your single best piece of content. Story-driven. |
| #3 (Day 4) | Social proof | Social Proof + Survivorship | Case study or testimonial. "Here's what happened when [customer] applied this." |
| #4 (Day 6) | Soft pitch | Anchoring + Framing | Introduce your product as the natural next step. |
| #5 (Day 8) | Hard pitch / community | Loss Aversion + In-Group | "Last chance at [offer]. Or join [community]." |

**Bias details per email:**
- **#1 Reciprocity:** Giving value first triggers obligation to return. Make the lead magnet genuinely valuable.
- **#2 Availability:** A vivid story embeds in memory. The reader will recall YOU when they recall the story.
- **#3 Survivorship:** Show your best customer outcome. Add credibility: "One of our top results."
- **#4 Anchoring:** Before stating price, anchor with "What it should cost" or "What others charge."
- **#5 Loss Aversion:** "Price increases after this email" / "Enrollment closes Friday."

---

### 2. NURTURE SEQUENCE (ongoing, value-first)

**Psychological arc:** Build authority → Maintain in-group identity → Occasional pitch.

**Cadence:** 3 value emails → 1 pitch email. The ratio prevents pitch fatigue.

| Type | Purpose | Dominant Bias |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Story/lesson | Authority + Availability | Personal story, lesson learned, mistake + fix |
| Framework/insight | Authority + Anchoring | "The [X]-step framework I use for [outcome]" |
| Curated roundup | Status Quo + In-Group | "5 links worth your time this week" |
| Pitch | Social Proof + Loss Aversion | Case study, limited offer, new product |

**Rules:**
- The pitch email should feel like a natural extension of the value emails - same voice, same quality.
- Never pitch without first establishing: (a) the problem, (b) why existing solutions fail, (c) your unique angle.
- The "curated roundup" format leverages Status Quo - it's a familiar format. Readers open it on habit.

---

### 3. CART ABANDONMENT (1–3 emails)

**Psychological arc:** Reminder → Social proof → Urgency/loss.

| Email | Timing | Bias | Content |
|-------|--------|------|---------|
| #1 | 1 hour after | Endowment | "Your [item] is waiting. Complete your order." |
| #2 | 24 hours after | Social Proof + Confirmation | "X people bought [item] today. Here's what they're saying." |
| #3 | 48–72 hours after | Loss Aversion + Scarcity | "Your cart expires in [time]. Price increases / stock is low." |

**Bias details:**
- **Endowment:** They already mentally "own" the item. The email reminds them it's theirs - they just haven't taken possession.
- **Social Proof:** Seeing others buy the SAME item validates the choice. "People like you are buying this right now."
- **Confirmation:** Show reviews specific to the abandoned item. Their interest is confirmed as correct.
- **Loss Aversion:** "Expiring" cart = losing something you almost had. More powerful than "complete your purchase."

---

### 4. RE-ENGAGEMENT (inactive subscribers, 3–5 emails)

**Psychological arc:** "We miss you" → Guilt (gently) → Value reminder → Final ultimatum → Goodbye.

| Email | Tone | Bias | Content |
|-------|------|------|---------|
| #1 | Warm | In-Group | "You've been quiet. Everything okay? Here's what you missed." |
| #2 | Curious | Sunk Cost + Cognitive Dissonance | "You signed up because [original reason]. Is that still a priority?" |
| #3 | Valuable | Reciprocity | Your single best piece of content. No pitch. Pure value. |
| #4 | Urgent | Loss Aversion | "Last chance. Reply 'yes' to stay, or we'll stop emailing." |
| #5 | Final | Closure (no bias needed) | "Goodbye. Here's how to find us if you ever need us." |

**Bias details:**
- **Cognitive Dissonance:** Remind them of the promise they made to themselves when they signed up. The gap between intention and action creates discomfort → engagement or unsubscription (both are outcomes you want).
- **Sunk Cost:** They've already invested time reading your past emails. Leaving now makes that past investment "wasted."
- **Reciprocity:** Before asking them to stay, GIVE something. Pure value. No strings.
- **Loss Aversion:** The "last chance" email should make them feel what they lose by leaving.

## SUBJECT LINE BIAS PATTERNS

| Bias | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Curiosity (Availability) | Unexpected statement | "I was wrong about [topic]" |
| Fear / Loss Aversion | Threat + solution | "This mistake is costing you $[X]/month" |
| Social Proof | Numbers | "Join 5,000+ who already [action]" |
| Anchoring | Specific value | "The $[X] lesson I learned in 2019" |
| In-Group | Exclusivity | "For [identity] only: [insider info]" |
| Cognitive Dissonance | Contradiction | "You say [value] matters. But..." |
| Scarcity / Urgency | Time limit | "Closes tonight: [offer]" |

**Subject line rules:**
- 30–50 characters for mobile (most opens are mobile).
- Avoid spam trigger words ("FREE", "BUY NOW", ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation!!!).
- The subject line's job is to get the OPEN. The preview text's job is to reinforce the reason to open. The first line's job is to hook.

---

## THE PSYCHOLOGICAL ARC OF A SEQUENCE

```
Trust → Desire → Justification → Action → Loyalty

Trust = Authority + Reciprocity + In-Group
Desire = Availability + Framing + Social Proof
Justification = Anchoring + Confirmation + Cognitive Dissonance resolution
Action = Loss Aversion + Scarcity + Risk Reversal
Loyalty = Endowment + Sunk Cost + Status Quo + In-Group
```

If you skip a stage, the sequence breaks. Someone who doesn't trust you won't feel desire. Someone who feels desire but can't justify the cost won't act. Someone who acts but doesn't feel smart about it won't stay.
