# Scenario: Cold Outreach (Email + DM)

## CONTEXT
Contacting someone who doesn't know you - cold email, LinkedIn DM, Twitter DM, or any unsolicited outreach - with the goal of starting a conversation that leads to a sale, partnership, or opportunity.

## GOAL
Survive the 3-second deletion window, earn a reply, and start a relationship. Cold outreach is the hardest marketing format because you have zero trust, zero social proof (to that recipient), and negative attention credit.

---

## THE COLD OUTREACH PARADOX

You cannot use your strongest marketing biases. Social Proof? They don't know your customers. Authority? They don't know you. In-Group? You're an outsider by definition. Sunk Cost? They've invested nothing.

**What's left:** Availability (pattern-interrupt), Fundamental Attribution Error (it's not them, it's the circumstance), Reciprocity (give first), and microscopic doses of Authority (one credible signal).

---

## STRUCTURE (5 lines max - the 5-Line Rule)

Every cold message must fit in a phone notification preview. If they have to click "see more," you've already lost 60% of recipients.

```
Line 1: PATTERN INTERRUPT - not a template they've seen 100 times.
Line 2: PERSONALIZATION - prove you know who they are.
Line 3: VALUE / INSIGHT - something they didn't know. Give first.
Line 4: LOW-FRICTION ASK - not a meeting. A reply. A click. One sentence.
Line 5: SIGN-OFF - name + one-line credibility signal.
```

---

## LINE-BY-LINE PSYCHOLOGY

### Line 1: Pattern Interrupt
```
[AVAILABILITY] Something unexpected enough to pause the scroll.
[FRAMING] Not about you. About something THEY care about.
```

**FAIL:** "Hi [Name], my name is [X] and I'm the founder of [Company]..."
**WHY:** Pattern-matched as "sales email" in 0.3 seconds. Deleted.

**WORKS:**
- "[Specific observation about their work/product/content]"
- "Your post about [topic] made me rethink [thing]."
- "I noticed [company] just [did something interesting]."
- "Question about your [specific project/launch/article]."

**Rules:**
- Never start with your name, your company, or "I'm reaching out."
- Start with THEM. Something specific. Something a template couldn't generate.
- The first line must prove you're human, you did homework, and this isn't mass-sent.

---

### Line 2: Personalization
```
[FUNDAMENTAL ATTRIBUTION ERROR] Attribute their success to skill, not luck.
[CONFIRMATION] Validate something they've done - they'll feel recognized.
```

**FAIL:** "I see you're the VP of Marketing at [Company]."
**WHY:** That's on their LinkedIn profile. You've proven you can read. Not impressive.

**WORKS:**
- "The campaign you ran for [client/product] was smart - especially [specific element]."
- "I've been following [Company]'s shift to [strategy]. Bold move."
- "Your thread on [topic] had a point nobody else made: [specific point]."

**Rules:**
- Reference something 90% of people wouldn't notice. Effort = credibility.
- Be specific. "I liked your post" = template. "The third point in your thread about retention - that's a pricing problem, not a product problem" = human.

---

### Line 3: Value / Insight (the "Give" - Reciprocity)
```
[RECIPROCITY] Give something before you ask for anything.
[AVAILABILITY] Make it specific and immediately useful.
[AUTHORITY] This line establishes your right to speak on this topic.
```

**FAIL:** "We help companies like yours increase revenue by 30%."
**WHY:** Claim without proof. In cold outreach, unsubstantiated claims are indistinguishable from spam.

**WORKS:**
- "I analyzed [their public data] and noticed [insight they might not have seen]."
- "We just published research on [topic relevant to them]. One finding: [specific data point]."
- "I solved a similar problem at [your company / for a client]. The fix was [specific approach]."
- "Here's something I built that might help: [link to genuinely useful free resource]."

**Rules:**
- The value must be specific, not generic. "Here's a tip" = 0. "We found that changing button color from blue to green increased conversions 11% - here's the test data" = 1.
- If attaching/linking, it must be immediately consumable. Not a 45-minute video. Not a 30-page PDF. A tweet-length insight or a 2-minute read.
- This is where you earn the right to ask in Line 4.

---

### Line 4: Low-Friction Ask
```
[STATUS QUO] The ask should feel like a tiny, natural next step.
[LOSS AVERSION FRAMING] Optional: "No worries if not" reduces pressure paradoxically increases response rate.
```

**FAIL:** "Would you be open to a 30-minute call next week to discuss how we can help?"
**WHY:** 30 minutes = high cognitive load + calendar anxiety + "this is a sales call." Most people would rather do almost anything else.

**WORKS (ranked by friction):**
1. **Reply to question:** "Curious - have you tried [approach] for [problem]?"
2. **Feedback ask:** "Would love your take on [specific thing]. 2 minutes?"
3. **Content offer:** "I wrote a deeper breakdown. Want me to send it?"
4. **Specific yes/no:** "Would [specific solution] work for [their situation]?"
5. **Call as last resort:** "If this is useful, happy to jump on a 10-minute call."

**Rules:**
- The "ask" should require the recipient to do ONE thing. Reply. Click. Not "think about whether to schedule."
- If you MUST ask for a call: 10 minutes, not 30. Specific topic, not "discuss how we can help."
- "No worries if not" in the ask line increases response rates. It removes pressure - the brain relaxes and says "sure, I can reply."

---

### Line 5: Sign-off + Credibility Signal
```
[AUTHORITY] One-line credibility. Not a bio. One signal.
```

**FAIL:** "John Smith, Founder & CEO of Acme Corp - we've helped 500+ companies, featured in Forbes, TechCrunch..."
**WHY:** You're a stranger. A list of accomplishments from a stranger reads as desperate, not impressive.

**WORKS:**
- "- [First Name], [One relevant role] at [Company]" (keep it minimal)
- "- [First Name]. (I wrote the [topic] guide that [credible person] shared last week.)"
- "- [First Name] at [Company]. We helped [well-known client] with [specific thing]."

**Rules:**
- One credibility signal. Not three. One strong signal > three weak ones.
- Signal should be relevant to the recipient. "Featured in Forbes" is irrelevant to a developer. "Built the authentication system at Stripe" is relevant.
- If you have no credibility signal, don't fake one. Just sign your name. Authenticity > inflated authority.

---

## FOLLOW-UP PSYCHOLOGY

**The Rule of 3:**
- Email 1: Value-first. No pitch. ("I noticed X. Here's an insight.")
- Email 2 (3–5 days later): Reference Email 1. Add value. Soft pitch. ("Following up on my note about X. Here's another data point. Would [specific solution] be useful?")
- Email 3 (7–10 days later): The break-up. ("I assume the timing isn't right. If [problem] becomes a priority, here's how to reach me.")

**Why 3 and not more:** After 3 unanswered emails, you're not "persistent." You're "annoying." The break-up email respects their time and leaves the door open. Many replies come on Email 3.

**Follow-up subject line hack:** "Re: [original subject]" - the "Re:" makes it look like an ongoing conversation, not a cold pitch. Opens increase significantly.

---

## COLD OUTREACH BIAS STACK
```
Availability (pattern interrupt - specific, unexpected observation)
 → Confirmation (validate something they did - they feel seen)
   → Reciprocity (give value first - insight, data, resource)
     → Status Quo (low-friction ask - one tiny next step)
       → Authority (one credibility signal - relevant, minimal)
```

**Biases you CANNOT use in cold outreach:**
- Social Proof: "Join 10,000 others" - they don't care. They're not in the 10,000.
- Scarcity / Urgency: "Limited time offer" - immediate delete. Feels like spam.
- Sunk Cost: They've invested nothing. Not applicable.
- In-Group: You're an outsider until they reply.

---

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC RULES

### Cold Email
- Subject line: ≤ 5 words. Curiosity or specificity, not "Quick question" (the most overused, ignored subject line in history).
- Send Tuesday–Thursday, 6–9 AM recipient's time zone.
- Plain text > HTML. It feels personal, not automated.
- No images. No tracking pixels (privacy-aware recipients flag these).
- No attachments in Email 1 (spam filters).

### LinkedIn DM
- Connection request note: ≤ 300 characters. Same 5-line structure compressed.
- "I follow your work on [topic]. I noticed [specific observation]. Would love to connect."
- After connection: wait 24 hours. Then send the full outreach message.
- Never pitch in the connection request.

### Twitter/X DM
- Only possible if they follow you or have DMs open. Don't pitch in public replies.
- Even shorter: 2–3 lines max. Twitter DMs are casual by nature.
- "Your thread on X was great. One thing I'd add: [insight]."
