# Scenario: Webinar Warmup

## CONTEXT
Running a webinar funnel: pre-event promotion, the live event itself, and post-event follow-up to convert attendees into buyers.

## GOAL
Fill seats with qualified leads, deliver a high-converting presentation, and close sales in the 24–72 hour post-webinar window.

---

## PHASE 1: REGISTRATION PROMOTION (7–14 days before)

### Bias Stack
`Fear (problem awareness) → Authority (who's teaching) → Social Proof (who's attending) → Scarcity (limited seats or replay access)`

### Content Types

**1. The Problem Hook (Social + Email)**
> "The #1 reason [audience] fail at [outcome] isn't what you think."
- *Primary bias: Fear/Loss Aversion* - you're losing and don't know why.
- *Secondary: Availability* - concrete, relatable failure scenario.

**2. The Credibility Builder**
> "I've spent [X years] / [$X dollars] figuring this out. In 60 minutes, I'll show you what I found."
- *Primary bias: Authority + Anchoring* - the investment anchor makes the free webinar feel disproportionately valuable.
- *Secondary: Sunk Cost (for YOU)* - your past investment is proof of expertise.

**3. Social Proof Enrollment**
> "2,300+ [role/identity] have already registered. Here's what they're saying: [testimonial from past attendee]."
- *Primary bias: Social Proof + Bandwagon* - "people like me are doing this."
- *Secondary: False Consensus* - registrants assume their peers are also interested.

**4. Scarcity Injection (2–3 days before)**
> "Replay available for 48 hours only. Live attendees get [bonus] that won't be in the replay."
- *Primary bias: Scarcity + Loss Aversion* - "If I don't attend live, I lose the bonus."
- *Secondary: In-Group* - live attendees are the "inner circle."

## PHASE 2: THE WEBINAR (60–90 min live)

### The Psychological Arc (minute-by-minute)

| Time | Segment | Bias | What happens in the brain |
|------|---------|------|---------------------------|
| 0:00–0:05 | Hook + Promise | Availability + Framing | "This matters. I'm in the right place." |
| 0:05–0:15 | Your Story | Authority + Bias Blind Spot | "This person is credible AND relatable." |
| 0:15–0:35 | The Method | Framing + Anchoring | "This isn't just advice. This is a SYSTEM." |
| 0:35–0:50 | Case Study / Proof | Social Proof + Survivorship | "It worked for them. It can work for me." |
| 0:50–1:05 | The Offer | Anchoring + Framing | "At this price, it's a no-brainer." |
| 1:05–1:15 | Stack + Bonuses | Anchoring + Endowment | "The bonuses alone are worth more than the price." |
| 1:15–1:30 | Q&A + Close | Scarcity + Loss Aversion | "I need to act NOW or I'll miss this." |

### Detailed Bias Application

**Hook (0:00–0:05):**
Open with a story (Availability) of someone exactly like your audience who struggled with the problem - and then solved it with what you're about to teach. Do NOT introduce yourself yet. The story IS the hook.

**Your Story (0:05–0:15):**
Tell your personal journey. Include your FAILURES. This is the Bias Blind Spot in action: admitting your own biases and mistakes makes you infinitely more trustworthy than someone who's only been right. "I believed [wrong thing] for years. It cost me [concrete loss]. Then I discovered [insight]."

**The Method (0:15–0:35):**
Present a named framework or system. Name it. Brand it. "The [X] Method" / "The [Y] Framework." The name = the anchor. The method = the framing. Teach real value - don't hold back. The more value you give, the more credible the offer becomes (Reciprocity).

**Case Study (0:35–0:50):**
Show one detailed case study: before, the struggle, the application of your method, the after. Use specific numbers, dates, and names. Survivorship Bias: show your BEST result. Acknowledge it's a top result for credibility. "This is one of our best outcomes, and here's exactly what made it work."

**The Offer (0:50–1:05):**
Reveal the offer. Start with WHAT THEY GET. Then, before revealing price, anchor: "Consulting with me 1-on-1 costs $X. Programs like this typically cost $Y." Then reveal YOUR price. The gap between anchor and price triggers the "this is a deal" response.

**Stack + Bonuses (1:05–1:15):**
Add bonuses one by one with assigned dollar values. Each bonus: name + value + what it solves. "Bonus #1: [name] (value: $X). This alone solves [pain point]." The total stack value should be 3–5× the offer price. The stack is the justification engine for System 2.

**Q&A + Close (1:15–1:30):**
Answer questions. Each Q&A is an opportunity for social proof ("Great question - a lot of people ask this"). Then final close: urgency (limited-time bonus, limited spots for personalized support, price increase warning). Loss Aversion + Scarcity.

## PHASE 3: POST-WEBINAR SEQUENCE (72 hours)

### Email Sequence

| Email | Timing | Bias | Content |
|-------|--------|------|---------|
| #1 | Immediately after | Reciprocity + Endowment | "Here's the replay + your bonus worksheet." Give value. No pitch. |
| #2 | Next morning | Social Proof + Confirmation | "Here's what attendees are saying." Show testimonials from the live chat. |
| #3 | 24h after | Sunk Cost + Anchoring | "You invested 90 minutes. Don't let that go to waste. Here's the one thing to do next." |
| #4 | 48h after | Loss Aversion + Scarcity | "Replay comes down in 24h. Bonus expires. Price increases." |
| #5 | 72h after | Final Loss Aversion | "Last chance. The offer disappears at midnight." |

**The Replay Rule:** Replay should genuinely be limited. "Replay available for 48 hours" means 48 hours. Artificial scarcity that's exposed destroys all future trust. Real scarcity creates real urgency.

---

## BIAS CHECKLIST FOR WEBINAR
- [ ] Hook is a story, not a biography
- [ ] Personal story includes failures and wrong beliefs (Bias Blind Spot)
- [ ] Method has a NAME (Anchoring)
- [ ] Method taught with real value - not holding back (Reciprocity)
- [ ] Case study is specific: names, dates, numbers (Survivorship + Availability)
- [ ] Price anchored against higher reference points before revealed
- [ ] Bonus stack exceeds offer value by 3–5×
- [ ] Urgency is genuine - real deadlines, real limited bonuses
- [ ] Post-webinar sequence feeds confirmation bias (you made the right choice attending)
- [ ] Final CTA leverages sunk cost (you've invested time watching) + loss aversion (this offer disappears)
