# Scenario: Ad Campaign

## CONTEXT
Creating paid advertising - video ads (TikTok, YouTube, Reels), search ads (Google, Bing), display/retargeting, and social feed ads (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn).

## GOAL
Stop the scroll, capture attention in <3 seconds, communicate value in <15 seconds, and drive a measurable action - click, sign-up, or purchase.

---

## PLATFORM × BIAS × FORMAT

### Video Ads (TikTok, Reels, YouTube Shorts - ≤60s)

**The Brain's Ad Processing:**
- Second 0–3: Amygdala scan - "Threat? Opportunity? Ignore?"
- Second 3–8: System 1 engagement - "This feels relevant/familiar/exciting."
- Second 8–30: Value communication - "What's in it for me?"
- Second 30–60: Action trigger - "What do I do now?"

**Pattern A: The Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA (30–60s)**

| Time | What | Bias |
|------|------|------|
| 0–3s | **HOOK.** Bold statement, contradiction, fear trigger, or absurd visual. | Availability / Fear / Framing |
| 3–8s | **PROBLEM AGITATION.** "You know that feeling when [vivid scenario]?" | Availability / Loss Aversion |
| 8–20s | **SOLUTION.** Your product as the hero. Show it working. | Framing / Halo Effect |
| 20–30s | **PROOF.** Before/after, testimonial, stat, demo. | Social Proof / Survivorship |
| 30–45s | **OFFER.** Price, bonus, guarantee. Anchored against higher value. | Anchoring / Risk Reversal |
| 45–60s | **CTA.** "Click the link. Only [X] available. [Deadline]." | Loss Aversion / Scarcity |

**Pattern B: The Native/UGC Style (15–30s)**
Start in media res - like a regular person's video. No intro. No branding for 5 seconds.
- "I tried [thing] so you don't have to..."
- "My [role] friend showed me this and..."
- "Nobody's talking about this, but..."

*Primary bias: Availability + False Consensus.* The "regular person" format bypasses ad skepticism. Halo effect from relatability, not polish.

**Pattern C: The "They're All Wrong" (15–30s)**
Contrarian hook. Polemic.
- "Everything you've been told about [topic] is backwards."
- "Stop doing [common advice]. Here's why."

*Primary bias: Framing + Confirmation.* Dual effect: believers feel validated (dopamine), skeptics get curious (cognitive dissonance). Either way = engagement.

### Search Ads (Google/Bing - text only)

Search ads meet the user at the moment of intent. The psychology is different: they're already looking. Your job is to be the OBVIOUS choice, not to create desire from scratch.

| Query Intent | Bias Strategy | Ad Copy Approach |
|-------------|---------------|------------------|
| "best X for Y" | Authority + Social Proof | "Rated #1 by [authority]. Trusted by [number] teams." |
| "X vs Y" | Anchoring + Framing | "X costs $[anchor]. Get [your product] for $[price]. Same result, less cost." |
| "buy X" | Social Proof + Loss Aversion | "Join [number] happy customers. Limited stock. Free shipping." |
| "how to [solve problem]" | Availability + Authority | "The [timeframe] method for [result]. Free guide →" |
| "X reviews" | Social Proof + Backfire Effect | "Rated 4.9/5 from [number] reviews. See why →" |

**Headline rules:**
- Include the keyword (relevance = trust signal)
- Use numbers where possible ("3 steps", "10,000+ users", "4.9 stars")
- Headline 1 = value prop. Headline 2 = social proof or urgency. Headline 3 = CTA.
- Description: expand the headline. Include a specific outcome. End with CTA.

### Social Feed Ads (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)

**The Scroll-Stop Rule:** If the first frame doesn't make them pause in <1 second, nothing else matters.

| Element | Bias | Rule |
|---------|------|------|
| Visual (image/video still) | Halo Effect + Availability | Unusual, emotional, or curiosity-inducing. NOT generic stock. |
| Primary text (first 2 lines before "see more") | Framing + Fear / Loss Aversion | Hook them. The "see more" cut is the gate. Make them click. |
| Headline (bold text below image) | Social Proof + Anchoring | "The [adjective] way to [outcome]." |
| CTA button | Risk Reversal | "Learn More" (low risk) > "Shop Now" (higher psychological barrier). |

**Platform-Specific Psychology:**

**Facebook / Instagram (B2C):**
- Visual = 80% of ad performance. Faces > objects. Emotion > information.
- Emotional range: aspiration (luxury), relief (problem solved), belonging (community).
- Tone: `warm-human` or `bold-sell` depending on product.
- Best biases: Social Proof, Availability, Halo Effect, Endowment.

**LinkedIn (B2B):**
- Visual = professional but unusual. Data visualizations, framework diagrams, speaker photos.
- Emotional range: fear of falling behind, desire for status/advancement.
- Tone: `expert-calm` with sharp edges.
- Best biases: Authority, Anchoring, Loss Aversion, Sunk Cost.

**TikTok / Reels (B2C, younger):**
- Visual = native. Shot on phone > studio production. Imperfect = authentic.
- Emotional range: humor, surprise, outrage, curiosity.
- Tone: `rebel-edgy` or `warm-human`.
- Best biases: Availability, False Consensus, Social Proof.

### Retargeting Ads (Display + Social)

The prospect has already engaged. They've visited your site, read content, maybe added to cart. Retargeting is NOT about introduction. It's about REMINDING + JUSTIFYING + URGENCY.

| Stage | Bias | Message |
|-------|------|---------|
| Viewed product | Endowment | "Still thinking about [product]? It's waiting for you." |
| Viewed pricing | Anchoring + Social Proof | "X people bought [product] today. Here's why." |
| Abandoned cart | Loss Aversion + Sunk Cost | "You're so close. Complete your order before [deadline / stock runs out]." |
| Content reader (no product view) | Reciprocity + Authority | "Liked the article? Here's the tool that makes it actionable." |
| Past customer | Status Quo + In-Group | "You're part of [community]. Here's what's new / what you're missing." |

**Retargeting rules:**
- Frequency cap: max 3–5 impressions per user per week. More = annoyance = brand damage.
- Creative rotation: same ad shown 5 times = ignored. Rotate creatives with same message, different execution.
- Burn code: if user converted, REMOVE them from retargeting immediately. Nothing feels more incompetent than an ad for something they already bought.

## AD COPY FRAMEWORK (universal, any format)

### The 4-Part Ad Structure

```
1. HOOK (first line / first frame / headline)
   What they FEEL right now. The pain, the desire, the fear, the curiosity.
   Bias: Availability / Framing / Fear

2. PROBLEM / OPPORTUNITY (next 1–2 sentences)
   Why this matters now. Quantify if possible.
   Bias: Loss Aversion / Anchoring

3. SOLUTION (middle of the ad)
   Your product. But framed as the natural answer to the problem above.
   Bias: Framing / Authority / Halo Effect

4. ACTION (last line / CTA)
   What to do + why NOW + what they risk by waiting.
   Bias: Loss Aversion / Social Proof / Scarcity
```

## TESTING AND OPTIMIZATION (bias-driven)

### What to A/B Test

| Element | Hypothesis | Bias Being Tested |
|---------|------------|-------------------|
| Loss vs Gain headline | "Stop losing X" vs "Start gaining Y" | Loss Aversion |
| Specific vs Vague number | "10,247 users" vs "Thousands of users" | Social Proof specificity |
| First-person vs Third-person | "I tried this" vs "He tried this" | Availability (personal > abstract) |
| Urgency vs No urgency | "Offer ends tonight" vs no deadline | Loss Aversion + Scarcity |
| Before/After vs Product shot | Transformation vs Product | Survivorship + Availability |
| Social proof placement | Opening vs Closing | Trust sequence (trust first vs trust last) |

## BUDGET ALLOCATION BY BIAS POTENTIAL

| Campaign Goal | Best Biases | Ad Format | Measurement |
|---------------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Brand awareness | Availability, Halo Effect | Video, Display | Impressions, recall lift |
| Lead generation | Authority, Loss Aversion, Reciprocity | Search, Social | CPL, lead quality |
| Direct sales (low price) | Social Proof, Scarcity, Loss Aversion | Social, Retargeting | ROAS, CPA |
| Direct sales (high price) | Authority, Anchoring, Risk Reversal | Search, Long-form video | CPA, demo→sale rate |
| Retention / LTV | In-Group, Status Quo, Endowment | Email, Retargeting | Churn rate, repeat purchase rate |
