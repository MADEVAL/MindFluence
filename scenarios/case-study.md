# Scenario: Case Study / Success Story

## CONTEXT
A documented story of a real customer who achieved a specific result using your product or method. Used as social proof on sales pages, in emails, on social media, and in pitch decks.

## GOAL
Make the reader think "If they can do it, I can do it" - then take the next step toward purchase. A case study is the single most powerful piece of marketing content because it's simultaneously a story (System 1), a proof point (System 2), and a mirror for the reader's aspiration.

---

## THE CASE STUDY STRUCTURE (6-part arc)

### PART 1: SITUATION (50–100 words)
```
[AVAILABILITY] Introduce a specific, real person with concrete details.
[FALSE CONSENSUS] Frame their situation as typical for your target audience.
```

**Pattern:** "[Full Name] is a [Role] at [Company], [Location]. Like most [role/industry], they were dealing with [common situation]."

**Rules:**
- Full name, real photo. Anonymous case studies have zero credibility.
- The situation must mirror your target reader's situation. If the reader can't see themselves, they can't see the result as relevant.
- Include a humanizing detail: "Sarah, a mother of two," "Marcus, who taught himself to code at 34."

---

### PART 2: PROBLEM (100–200 words)
```
[FEAR / LOSS AVERSION] Describe the pain in specific, quantified terms.
[CONFIRMATION] Validate that the problem was real and frustrating.
[AVAILABILITY] Use sensory details to make the problem vivid.
```

**Pattern:** "But [specific problem] was holding them back. Every [time period], they were losing [quantified loss]. They tried [alternative 1] - [why it failed]. They tried [alternative 2] - [why it failed]. Nothing worked."

**Rules:**
- Quantify the problem. "Sales were stagnant" = weak. "Revenue had been flat at $12K/month for 14 months" = strong.
- Name the failed alternatives specifically. This creates contrast for your solution.
- Use the customer's own words if possible: "In Sarah's words: 'I felt like...'"

---

### PART 3: DISCOVERY (50–100 words)
```
[AVAILABILITY] How they found your product - the "meet-cute" moment.
[SOCIAL PROOF] If they found you via referral, name the referrer.
[CONFIRMATION] Their initial skepticism - and why they decided anyway.
```

**Pattern:** "Then [how they discovered you]. [Name] was skeptical at first - [specific doubt]. But [specific reason] convinced them to try."

**Rules:**
- Acknowledge initial skepticism. It makes the eventual success more credible.
- If referred, name the referrer - borrowed trust = instant authority.
- The discovery moment should feel authentic, not scripted. "A colleague forwarded an article" > "They were searching for solutions."

---

### PART 4: SOLUTION / IMPLEMENTATION (100–200 words)
```
[FRAMING] Describe your product as the mechanism of change, not a magic pill.
[STATUS QUO] Emphasize how easy or natural the transition was.
[AUTHORITY] Mention any support, guidance, or resources they received.
```

**Pattern:** "[Name] started using [Product / Method]. Specifically, they [specific action 1], then [specific action 2]. The key difference from what they'd tried before: [mechanism]. Within [timeframe], they noticed [early win]."

**Rules:**
- Show the process, not just the result. Process = believability. "They used our framework for 90 days" > "And then magic happened."
- Include an early win - a small result that proved it was working. Early wins build narrative momentum.
- The solution section is where you subtly differentiate from competitors. "Unlike [alternative], this approach [key difference]."

---

### PART 5: RESULTS (100–200 words)
```
[SURVIVORSHIP BIAS] Show the outcome - quantified, specific, verifiable.
[SOCIAL PROOF] The result IS the proof.
[CONFIRMATION] Frame the result as "what happens when [right approach] meets [right person]."
```

**Pattern:** "After [timeframe]: [Metric 1]: Before X → After Y ([%] change). [Metric 2]: Before X → After Y. [Metric 3 if available]. Beyond the numbers, [qualitative impact]: [quote about how it changed their life/business]."

**Rules:**
- Minimum 2 quantified results. 3 is ideal.
- Show percentage change AND absolute numbers. "$10K to $25K (150% increase)" = complete picture.
- Include one qualitative result: "And she now spends Fridays with her kids."
- Never fabricate numbers. A real case study with modest results outperforms a fake one with spectacular results.

---

### PART 6: TESTIMONIAL QUOTE + CTA (50–100 words)
```
[SOCIAL PROOF] Direct quote from the customer.
[CONFIRMATION] The quote should validate the reader's desire.
[AVAILABILITY] Natural, conversational language - not marketing-speak.
```

**Pattern:** > "[Direct quote from customer, 2–4 sentences. Should mention a specific result and an emotional payoff.]"
>
> - [Full Name], [Role] at [Company]

**Then a soft CTA:** "Want results like [Name]'s? [Link: See how / Start your trial / Book a call]."

**Rules:**
- The quote must sound like a real person, not a marketing department. If the customer didn't say it, don't put it in quotes.
- A good quote includes: specific result + emotional payoff. "We grew 40% AND I finally sleep through the night."
- CTA should match the reader's temperature. Cold reader: "See how it works." Hot reader: "Start now."

---

## BIAS SEQUENCE
```
Availability (Situation - real person, real details)
 → Fear / Loss (Problem - quantified pain)
   → Availability + Social Proof (Discovery)
     → Framing + Status Quo (Solution - here's how easy it was)
       → Survivorship + Social Proof (Results - quantified outcome)
         → Social Proof + Confirmation (Testimonial quote)
           → Bias-informed CTA
```

## FORMAT VARIANTS

### Short-form (for social media, emails)
Situation (1 sentence) → Problem (1 sentence) → Solution (1 sentence) → Result (1 sentence with number) → Quote (1 line) → CTA.

### Medium-form (for landing pages, pitch decks)
Situation (paragraph) → Problem (2 paragraphs) → Discovery (1 paragraph) → Solution (2 paragraphs) → Results (2 paragraphs with metrics) → Quote → CTA.

### Long-form (for sales pages, case study pages)
Full structure above. Add visuals: charts, before/after screenshots, photos of the customer.

### Video case study (for ads, testimonials)
3–5 minutes. Customer speaks directly to camera. Use the 6-part structure as interview questions. Edit tightly - keep only the strongest moments.

---

## RULES FOR CREDIBILITY
- Real names, real companies, real numbers. Period.
- If numbers are approximate, say "approximately" or "~".
- If the customer gave permission in writing, use their photo. Face = trust.
- Never put words in a customer's mouth. Use their actual quotes or paraphrase with "in their words."
- If the result is exceptional, acknowledge it: "This is one of our strongest outcomes." Honesty about selection bias = more trust.
