# Scenario: Landing Page

## CONTEXT
Designing the structure and copy for a landing page - the single page that converts visitors into leads or customers.

## GOAL
Maximize conversion rate by engineering the psychological journey from "stranger" to "buyer" using a deliberate sequence of cognitive biases.

---

## THE PSYCHOLOGICAL JOURNEY (above the fold to footer)

The human brain makes the buy/don't-buy decision in ~50ms of landing, then spends the rest of the page justifying that snap judgment. Your job: make the 50ms decision "YES" and then feed the justification machine.

---

## SECTION-BY-SECTION BIAS MAP

### 1. HERO (above the fold - decides if they stay)
```
[FRAMING] Headline: name the problem OR state the transformation.
[LOSS AVERSION] Subhead: what they're losing by NOT having this.
[SOCIAL PROOF] 3–5 recognizable logos or a single stat ("Used by 10,000+ teams").
[RISK REVERSAL] CTA: "Start free trial" / "Get started - no credit card."
[HALO EFFECT] Hero visual: premium, clean, emotionally resonant.
```

**Rules:**
- Headline MUST pass the "5-second test" - a stranger should understand the value proposition in 5 seconds.
- Never use vague headlines like "Revolutionize Your Workflow." Use specific transformation or named pain.
- CTA button text should complete the sentence "I want to..." - "Start my free trial", "See my options", "Get the report."
- Hero image/video quality IS your product quality in the visitor's mind. Compress nothing. Compromise nothing.

---

### 2. PROBLEM AGITATION (2–3 paragraphs)
```
[FEAR / LOSS AVERSION] Make the problem vivid, specific, and personal.
[AVAILABILITY] Use a story or scenario the reader recognizes immediately.
[ANCHORING] Quantify the cost of the problem. "This is costing you $X per month."
```

**Pattern:** "You know that feeling when [vivid scenario]? That's not just annoying. It's costing you [quantified loss]. And it gets worse: [escalation of pain]."

---

### 3. SOLUTION INTRODUCTION (2–3 paragraphs)
```
[FRAMING] Introduce your product not as "a tool" but as a category of one.
[AUTHORITY] Why you built this / why you're uniquely qualified.
[HALO EFFECT] Show the product in its best light - screenshots, mockups, video.
```

**Pattern:** "That's why we built [product]. Not another [crowded category]. A [new category] that [specific mechanism of solving the problem]."

---

### 4. HOW IT WORKS (3 steps max)
```
[STATUS QUO] Each step reduces perceived difficulty of change.
[AVAILABILITY] Use visual or concrete metaphors, not abstract descriptions.
```

**Pattern:** "Step 1: [simple action]. Step 2: [simple action]. Step 3: [result]. That's it."

Fewer steps = lower perceived effort = higher conversion. Never exceed 4 steps.

---

### 5. SOCIAL PROOF (the conversion engine)
```
[SOCIAL PROOF] Testimonials with: face photo, full name, role, specific result.
[CONFIRMATION] Testimonials should echo the beliefs your target audience already holds.
[SURVIVORSHIP BIAS] Show your best outcomes. Acknowledge they're top results for credibility.
[FALSE CONSENSUS] Group testimonials by persona. "If you're a [role], here's what your peers say."
```

**Formats ranked by power:**
1. Video testimonial (face + voice + emotion)
2. Photo + name + role + specific metric ("Grew revenue 34% in 90 days")
3. Photo + name + role + qualitative quote
4. Text-only quote (weakest - use only as filler)

**The "Wall of Love" pattern:** A dense grid of positive tweets/short quotes. The sheer volume = social proof.

---

### 6. FEATURES AS BENEFITS
```
[FRAMING] Every feature described as a BENEFIT, linked to a PAIN POINT.
[ENDOWMENT] Use "Your" language: "Your dashboard", "Your analytics."
[ANCHORING] Compare to the "old way" for contrast.
```

**Format for each feature:**
```
Feature name → What it does → Why it matters to the reader → What happens WITHOUT it
```

Never list features as bullet points of capabilities. Transform every capability into a reader outcome.

---

### 7. PRICING
```
[ANCHORING] Three tiers. Highest first (visually). Middle = "Most Popular" (highlighted).
[DECOY] The highest tier exists primarily to make the middle tier feel like a bargain.
[LOSS AVERSION] Annual billing: show monthly equivalent. "Save $X/year" (loss frame).
[RISK REVERSAL] "30-day money-back guarantee", "Cancel anytime."
[SCARCITY] If genuine: "Only X spots at this price", "Price increases after launch."
```

**Pricing table rules:**
- Middle column MUST be visually distinct (different color, "MOST POPULAR" badge, shadow/elevation).
- The "free" tier (if any) serves as lead generation, not a real option for serious users.
- The "enterprise" tier anchors everything upward.
- Show savings in absolute numbers, not percentages. "$200 saved" > "20% off."

---

### 8. FAQ / OBJECTION HANDLING
```
[BACKFIRE EFFECT] Validate the concern. Then transcend it.
[COGNITIVE DISSONANCE] Resolve contradictions the reader may feel.
[STATUS QUO] Address risk of switching. "What about my existing setup?"
```

**Pattern for each FAQ:**
- Question: phrased as the visitor would actually ask it (not sanitized marketing-speak).
- Answer: "Yes, and..." or "I understand why you'd think that. Here's the reality:"

**Essential FAQs to address:**
- Price objection ("Why is this worth $X?")
- Comparison ("How is this different from [competitor]?")
- Risk ("What if it doesn't work for me?")
- Switching cost ("I already use [competitor]. Why switch?")
- Time to value ("How long until I see results?")

---

### 9. FINAL CTA
```
[URGENCY] Time-limited element if genuine.
[SOCIAL PROOF] "Join X,000+ [customers/teams/users]."
[RISK REVERSAL] Repeat the guarantee.
[SUNK COST] "You've read this far. Last step."
```

**Pattern:** One clear action. No navigation. No alternative links. No "maybe later." The final CTA is a conversion singularity.

---

## LANDING PAGE BIAS SEQUENCE (full journey)
```
Framing (hero)
  → Fear/Loss (problem agitation)
    → Framing (solution intro)
      → Status Quo (how it works - easy)
        → Social Proof (testimonials)
          → Confirmation (they're smart to choose this)
            → Framing (features → benefits)
              → Anchoring (pricing)
                → Backfire Effect (FAQ - concerns validated, then resolved)
                  → Loss Aversion + Social Proof + Risk Reversal (final CTA)
```

---

## CONVERSION KILLERS (what NOT to do)
- Generic headlines ("Welcome to the future of...")
- Feature lists without benefit translation
- Anonymous testimonials ("- John D.")
- Navigation links that leak visitors away from the conversion path
- Multiple CTAs that compete for attention
- Fake urgency (countdown that resets on reload)
- Stock photos of people who are clearly not your actual customers
- Jargon that your audience doesn't use in their own heads
