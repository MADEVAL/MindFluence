# Example: Video Ad Script (30 seconds)

**Context:** A language learning app that uses AI conversation practice.
**Request:** "Write a 30-second video ad script for US market. Target: 25–40 year olds who've tried Duolingo but still can't speak."

---

[TONE: rebel-edgy × warm-human]
[BIASES ENGAGED: Fear/Loss Aversion (#5), Sunk Cost (#12), Cognitive Dissonance (#8), Availability Heuristic (#6), Social Proof (#1), Halo Effect (#16), Bias Blind Spot (#19)]
[TARGET ACTION: Download app → start free trial]

---

**VISUAL** | **AUDIO**
---|---
[Close-up: person staring at phone, frustrated] | "847 days."
[Text on screen: "847-day streak"] | "That's how long I've been learning Spanish."
[Cut: person trying to order coffee in Spanish. Barista looks confused.] | "You know how many conversations I've actually had?"
[Beat. Silence.] | "Zero."
[Cut: friendly, direct-to-camera. Bright natural light. Real apartment background.] | "Streaks don't teach you to speak. Conversations do."
[App screen: AI avatar having a conversation. Natural back-and-forth. Subtitles optional.] | "This is Lango. You talk. It talks back. No matching with strangers. No scheduling. No judgment."
[Screen: 15 min/day → confident speaker. Real user testimonials flash: "Finally ordered in French!" — Sarah, 34] | "15 minutes a day. In 30 days, you'll have your first real conversation."
[Screen: Download on App Store / Google Play. "Free 7-day trial."] | "847 days in, I finally uninstalled the streak counter. Try Lango free for 7 days."
[Logo + tagline: "Lango. Talk like you mean it."] | "If you don't speak more in 30 days, it's free. Link below."

---

[RATIONALE]
- **Fear / Loss Aversion (opening):** "847 days" — a huge number. The viewer projects: "That's years of my life. What if I'm wasting the same time?" The fear is not "you'll fail at Spanish." It's "you're already failing and don't realize the cost."
- **Sunk Cost:** The 847-day streak is the sunk cost. The viewer who has a Duolingo streak immediately sees themselves. The ad doesn't attack the streak — it reframes it as misallocated effort. The sunk cost isn't wasted; it's just pointed in the wrong direction.
- **Cognitive Dissonance (the "zero" reveal):** The gap between "847 days of learning" and "zero conversations" is painful. The viewer's brain demands resolution. The ad provides it: the problem isn't YOU, it's the METHOD. Fundamental Attribution Error in action.
- **Availability Heuristic:** The coffee-shop scenario is universally recognizable. Every language learner has fantasized about that first real conversation — and feared it. The visual embeds the fear + desire in a single, recallable scene.
- **Halo Effect (app demo):** The app screen is clean, modern, uncluttered. The AI avatar looks friendly, not robotic. The visual quality transfers to perceived product quality.
- **Social Proof:** The real user testimonial ("Finally ordered in French! — Sarah, 34") follows the format: specific outcome + real name + age (peer signal). It's placed at the moment of maximum interest (after showing the product).
- **Risk Reversal (close):** "If you don't speak more in 30 days, it's free." This is a double-bind: the guarantee removes purchase risk AND sets an expectation (you WILL speak more). The prospect who downloads is already psychologically committed to the outcome.
- **Bias Blind Spot (meta):** By acknowledging "I finally uninstalled the streak counter," the narrator admits their own past mistake. This makes the recommendation trustworthy. Someone who says "I was wrong for 847 days" has credibility that "I was always right" can't match.
