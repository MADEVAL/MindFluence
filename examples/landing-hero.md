# Example: Landing Page Hero Section

**Context:** A premium meal-prep delivery service for busy professionals.
**Request:** "Write the hero section for our landing page. Target: professionals earning $100K+ who don't have time to cook healthy."

---

[TONE: luxe-minimal × expert-calm]
[BIASES ENGAGED: Framing, Loss Aversion, Anchoring, Halo Effect, Status Quo, In-Group Favoritism]
[TARGET ACTION: Click "See Your Menu" → enter zip code]

---

**Headline:**
Dinner shouldn't cost you your evening. Or your health.

**Subhead:**
Chef-prepared meals, delivered fresh. Ready in 3 minutes. Designed by nutritionists for people whose calendar has no white space.

**Social Proof Bar:**
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.9/5 from 8,400+ reviews | As seen in Forbes, WSJ, Bon Appétit

**Primary CTA:**
[See Your Menu →]

**Secondary CTA:**
How it works ↓

**Hero Visual:**
[Overhead shot of 3 plated meals on a marble counter. Warm lighting. Minimal styling. Steam visible. No faces, no clutter, no text overlay.]

---

[RATIONALE]
- **Framing (headline):** The headline doesn't sell "meal delivery." It frames the choice as dinner-vs-evening OR dinner-vs-health. The frame makes the problem about TIME and HEALTH - the two scarcest resources for the target audience - not about food convenience. "Shouldn't cost you your evening" is loss-framed time.
- **Loss Aversion (subhead):** "Calendar has no white space" is a pain signal the audience recognizes. "Ready in 3 minutes" is the relief. The subhead names the identity ("people whose calendar...") - In-Group Favoritism. The reader thinks: "That's me."
- **Anchoring + Authority (subhead):** "Chef-prepared" + "nutritionists" - two authority signals. The implied anchor: this isn't mass-produced frozen food. This is restaurant-quality. The brain compares it to takeout ($20–40/meal + 30 min wait) rather than grocery shopping.
- **Social Proof (review bar):** Specific rating (4.9), specific volume (8,400+), and media logos. Three types of social proof in one line. The media logos transfer Halo Effect from respected publications.
- **Halo Effect (hero visual):** Marble counter, warm lighting, steam, minimal styling. These visual cues signal "premium" before a single word of body copy is read. The halo of the visual transfers to product quality perception.
- **Status Quo (dual CTAs):** "See Your Menu" = low-commitment. "How it works" = even lower. Both reduce perceived switching cost. The visitor isn't committing to a subscription - just looking. The psychological barrier is minimal.
- **In-Group Favoritism:** Every element - phrasing, visual aesthetic, reference points - is calibrated to the $100K+ professional identity. Someone outside the target will self-select out (which is intentional - you want qualified leads, not noise).
